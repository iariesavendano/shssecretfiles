PLACES WE OFTEN GO INSIDE THE UNIVERSITY

In this places we, Senior High School Students, often go for food, leisure, activities etc.

PICTURES OTHER PLACES

CARPIO HALL

The Home of the Bulacan State University Laboratory High School. We stayed in this building for 4 years, Grade 7 to Grade 10, the whole of our Junior High School Life. 
If we don't have our classes we often go to the sheds in front of the building.
We also go to its quadrangle where it consists of to courts, the futsal/basketball court and the volleyball court, where we play sports for trainings and practice for performances.

ROXAS HALL

The Building of the College of Education, the college who manages the Laboratory High School. We are currently having most of our classes in this building, starting from Grade 11 until today, Grade 12. 
We held most of our classes in the Roxas Hall rooftop, in the two laboratories if we have our Science Classes.

VALENCIA HALL

This building is mostly used for formal events for the Laboratory High School students like Proms, Balls, Recognition Events, Graduations, Seminars, and other special formal events.

FEDERIZO HALL

The home of the Colleges of Science and College of Arts and Letters. It is the oldest building in the University and that's why it is considered as haunted. The students go to this building because of its food stalls in its ground floor.

FOOD COURT

This is were we often go if we are hungry and we are looking for food not only because it is the closest to our building but also because of its cheap but delicious foods that are offered. 
This is also the home of the famous store of Ka Ek-Ek, in her store they have anything you ask for.

FOOD STALLS IN HOME ECONOMICS BUILDING

If the Food Court and Federizo Hall food stalls are full or closed, these stalls is where we go. These stalls offer Rice Meals, Tuhog-tuhog, Snacks and Drinks. 

HEROES PARK

If we don't have our classes and the Carpio Hall Quadrangle is full, we go here. We also held most of our practices for performances in this vicinity.
